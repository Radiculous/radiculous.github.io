The Mark

View image | gettyimages.com

For ads to be sticky, ad campaigns should be able to trigger emotions. In the advertising world, there is no such thing as a ‘negative’ response. All repsonses be it positive (laugher and happiness) or negative (anger and contempt) are acceptable. As long as there is a response. The ad that is sticky, gives traction.

View image | gettyimages.com

Most memorable ad campaigns open with negative emotions like confusion and perplexity, and then regain the viewers attention and trust by somewhat explaining what the viewer just saw.

The tug of war between positive and negative response is the formula for a memorable and sticky campaign.

Crafting a successful mark is a lot like conceptualizing a successful campaign. An interplay between the confusion and clarity essential. It is a constant push and pull between these 2 emotions which makes an identity hard to forget.

We base our identity on this trait and create a mark that is confusing yet clear.

On first glance, the mark seems strange, unreadable and imbalanced. The ‘A’s apex and ‘V’s legs overshoot the mark’s overall cap-height. Moreover, the crossbar of the “T” and the ‘I’s tittle have been converted into a ligature (a non-existent ligature at that).

But the longer you look at it, the clearer the mark becomes. The imbalance somewhat seems to balance themselves out, the ligature seems so natural and you slowly make out the word ‘Nativ’. Suddenly, the mark is making sense.

You feel a sense of relief and pat yourself on the back for figuring the puzzle out-making out the mark. It should be wrong but it looks right.

The process resets when you start to read the mark. The more you dissect it, the more confusing it gets but the more confusing it gets, the clearer it becomes and the better you feel. You get lost in the loop of confusion and clarity.

The mark is a paradox.

The Typeface

The mark is a modified version of Galano Grotesque.

A geometric sans serif that shares its structure with Futura, Avant Garde and Avenir. It exemplifies contemporary and functional. Its rigid lines exude authority and boldness. It is a powerful sans serif. Galano Grotesque is loud. Galano Grotesque is sturdy. Galano Grotesque is modern.

Although Galano Grotesque is a lot of things, we modified the typeface to drive home the point that though there are excellent out of the box solutions available, Nativ is an agency willing to customize the solution that is best tailored for your message.

Concept 2 : Spectrum of devices

Nativ digital : Putting the KNOW in techKNOWlogy.

View image | gettyimages.com

Messages require contexts in order to attain the best results. The mastery of the medium is as important as crafting the message itself. Some get their point across better on a billboard, while others shine brightest as an online ad.

Media used to mean, TV, Newspaper and radio. Now another term has surfaced, “New Media” or “Digital Media”.

‘New Media’ in its infancy used to mean your desktop computer. Having access to the internet meant that you had to sit down in front of your computer and click on a clunky mouse to navigate webpages that were no different than reading a book.

View image | gettyimages.com

Currently the term new media is taking on a whole new meaning. We now have videos streaming from our wrists, cameras, on our phones and watches that track our every move. Our pocket computers (mobile phones and tablets) would rival most super computers that launched the Apollo 11. We are literally carrying the world’s information right in our hands.

If someone told me 20 years ago that we would not have to worry about memorizing a ton of data from history, because everything you would need to know would be at your fingertips, I would have thought the person was insane.

View image | gettyimages.com

But here we are, desktop computers that are as powerful as mainframes; pieces of glass that are as powerful as most desktops; and a new category, wearables that can track basically our movement, our pulse and tell us when to stand up and move around.

Yet another tool in the media agency’s tool kit. Another tool that agencies yet to master and wield effectively. Only this time, digital media is constantly evolving. So we have to move with it and ride the wave.

The Mark

You can view the mark as a growth of knowledge or a miniaturization of devices. Our mark has 3 co-centric “n”s which can be seen 2 ways :

  1. growing outward (representing growth) — Increasing Knowledge or
  2. shrinking inward (representing simplification) — miniaturization — simplification.

the outer “n” represents the desktop screen, the middle “n” represents the mobile screen and the third “n” well, represents the newcomer to the technological realm, the wearable. Within the 3 screens is a thumb navigating all 3 screens with a swipe.

A spectrum of devices, a wealth of information, all within our fingertips.

Typography

Bauer Grotesk is a typeface of the art-deco era, though a close relative to Futura, what separates Bauer Grotesk from other art-deco typefaces is that it stands out for not being so strict with the geometric shapes, lending the design to be a bit warmer, more humanistic.

We selected Bauer Grotesk to be the primary typeface of the mark because of its simple, bold and timeless features.

It supports powerful mark but does not overpower it. The name is only 1/8th the size compared to the mark but notice that despite the size of the label, the typeface can still hold its own ground. Making the name still readable despite its minute size.

The typeface works wonderful with the mark when set in Extra Bold.
