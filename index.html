Skinnymamas — Brand Identity

I is Fit

The Concept: I is Fit, I is Slim

We understand that fitting back into those jeans seem like a lifetime ago. Skinnymamas is the brand that aims to help you get your groove back.

Embed from Getty Images

It is our goal to let you become a better version of yourself. We understand that keeping fit and staying healthy while juggling daily commitments can be a challenge.

Your body is not what it used to be, shedding a few pounds can seem more difficult than it is.

To separate Skinnymamas from the other weight-loss brands out there, we opt to look at the cliche and swim against it. At Skinnymamas, we don’t go with the flow, we create the waves.

The Concept

When you hear the brand “Skinnymamas” what comes to mind?

These images?

Exactly. That’s why we avoid stereotypes. It’s easy to draw something like this and pass it off as the mascot for the brand, but the project brief said that the brand should evokes wittiness.

The identity should be smart. It does not need to be too loud.

We opt for a more subtle and unassuming approach to our identity.

The idea of the identity is a play on I being fit and I am thin.

That is why the identity does not have a mascot. Instead, we focus on “I”.

The I is healthy
The I is thin
The I is slim
The I is fit.

We want the customer to realize that the I is them, without being outright about it.

Instead of going for a logomark, we opt to go with a logotype. It is an efficient way to communicate your message without letting the audience think. This is a form of branding that most fashion agencies employ.

Typography

Our chosen typeface is the superfamily, Rockeby made by MyCreativeLand. Its tall and slender structure lend itself to be compressed without sacrificing legibility.

For our body type, we employ Whitney Narrow, a subset of Whitney family by Hoefler & Co.

Color Palette

Energetic, lively and active &endash; were the main themes that we use to come up with the color scheme.

Lime green coupled with the darker green give the the brand a natural image.

Bright orange and lemon yellow evoke freshness and hype.

The salmon pink to connote femininity, the brand after all is intended for women aged 30 to 50.
