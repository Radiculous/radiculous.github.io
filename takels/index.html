Typography

If the voice is how we speak, the typography is how we want to be heard. Every font type, every style has a corresponding voice. Takels is a collective of different, eccentric but like-minded geniuses.

When auditioning appropriate typefaces for identity, what we look for are voice and tone. Does the typeface convey the right voice? Does it read the right way? How is the tone?

Is it authoritative and imposing or is it sweet and subtle? Does it whisper or scream?

All these nuances have to be considered before delving in and proposing a typeface.

We looked through a few other foundries before settling in with Hoefler’s Knockout Family.

When choosing typography based on different width it would help that they all come from 1 family to maintain consistency of the shapes and structure of the letterforms.

Hoefler’s Knockout was chosen for the base skeleton of the logotype, it has been stretched and modified to suit the underlying grid.

Hoefler’s Knockout packs a punch and is perfect for poster design. Knockout is based on Gothic woodtypes which existed a century before swiss Helvetica came into the scene.

Gothic woodtypes were common to old boxing posters which used woodtype instead of metal had a certain panachè and warmth.

Having letterforms carved out of wood offered it a certain flexibility when it came to widths. From the thin condensed to the bulkier bolds. Woodtype allows itself to be expressive without deviating much from the general form.

The letterforms have been painstakingly tweaked, kerned and redrawn to make sure they work together harmoniously. Delivering the message, inspiring action.

We tweak the letterforms and shape to fit the underlying Aspect Ratio Grid widths and kern to maintain its legibility despite the distortion.

Of course, not all typeface family can be used vanilla, it still needs to be tweaked, pushed and pulled to suit our messaging.
